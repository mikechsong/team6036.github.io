Join Us

As a community organization, Team 6036 prides itself in being extremely open: we take anyone who is interested in contributing to the technical education of themselves or others in the community, and no time commitment is required. This also means that no previous knowledge of anything robotics or management related is necessary to join -- you just have to be in high school. That said, few people can jump into robotics and learn every aspect of the field without serious time and effort. This is why we have subteams: specialized groups of members that work on a specific part of the necessary components of the robot. As a team, we are vaguely divided into Software, Build, Design, and Business. Note that you're not forced to be in a single one or any of these subteams to contribute, but they are organized and led separately, as their tasks are vastly different.

Interested?

If you are interested in one or more of our subteams, or robotics as a whole, feel free to email us! We'll be happy to answer any questions. If you're ready to apply, we'll send you an application form, and we'll review your application and respond with some next steps.

Software

Our tools include VS Code, Java, an frc-specific set of codebases that assist us with the more nitty-gritty parts of coding, and git among several other less crucial ones. These all have their learning curves, and every member is self taught in most or all of them. We know coding is hard, and we understand that it might be intimidating for people new to the subject. We're here to help anyone interested in learning to find success. Email the software captain at jlsatpa@gmail.com for any questions.

Build

As it is quite a lot of work, it is the largest subteam and some members choose to focus on certain aspects of build, such as operating the machine tools or working with electronics. In addition, members who usually work with other subteams often come to help get the robot finished on time.

Design

Design is sometimes hectic: we must consider cost, machinability, ease of assembly, and effectiveness, all within the first few weeks of game release. Understanding the best practices and learning the design software (Fusion360 and Solidworks) can be daunting, but we would be happy to provide the software licenses needed and get new members started.

Business

The work of the Business subteam is often done throughout the year, not necessarily at the same time as the construction of the robot in January through March. As such, the members of this subteam often also participate heavily in the other subteams, when the robot is being brainstormed, designed, built, wired, programmed, driven, and troubleshooted.
